What is this “question exclamation mark combo” symbol?

It’s called an interrobang (‽). Whether you call it a question-exclamation mark, an exclamation-question mark, or something else, it's a single punctuation mark that merges both the question mark and exclamation point.

How do I type it?

Most keyboards don’t have a direct key for it. That’s why this site provides a quick copy button—just click “Copy,” then paste wherever you’d like.
